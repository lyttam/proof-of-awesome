Interpersonal Strengths

  • Collaboration

    I value team consensus, especially when it comes to high-level decisions. As a member of a small agile team working to convert legacy JavaScript into reusable React components, I had daily discussions with my colleagues about architecture and code style that helped the transition go smoothly and ensured everyone was on the same page during the process.

  • Communication

    While I take pride in my written communication skills, nothing beats face-to-face interactions between team members. In my role as a technical lead on a project — with the majority of the team working from different remote locations — I arranged daily video chats as well as in-person meetings whenever feasible. As a result, productivity and team morale were vastly improved.

  • Mediation

    At work and in my personal life, I often find myself helping people from different backgrounds to better understand each other. I used my knowledge of both client and server-side technologies to bridge a gap between front-end and back-end developers on a project, helping each side to understand the other’s perspective and needs in order to get the job done efficiently and effectively.

  • Listening

    My rule of thumb when engaging with others is to do 30% talking and 70% active listening. I’m good at asking the right questions to make others feel comfortable opening up about themselves.

  • Mentoring

    I started mentoring at an early age: volunteering at a summer camp teaching survival skills to Girl Scouts, working at the YMCA as a swimming instructor, and teaching music lessons to kids. Those experiences laid the foundation for my successful on-the-job role as mentor for a summer intern, as well as constant two-way knowledge sharing with my peers.

  • Problem Solving

    The same methods I use to develop technical solutions are useful in personal situations as well. I’m level-headed and logical in the face of a challenge, able to see multiple sides of almost any conflict, and can outline rational arguments to help determine the best alternative.
